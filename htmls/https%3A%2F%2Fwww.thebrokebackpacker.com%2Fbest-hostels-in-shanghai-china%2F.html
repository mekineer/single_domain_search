What to Consider with the Best Hostels in Shanghai

When picking the best hostel in Shanghai (or anywhere in the world!), you should consider a few things…

  1. What is your travel style? A Digital Nomad traveling to Shanghai will have different needs from a couple traveling to Shanghai. Take into account what you need, and how you are traveling, then you can find a hostel that fits that.
  2. What is your budget? As more and more young people backpack and travel the world, the hostel scene gets better and better. Watching the developments in hostels worldwide over the past five years has been amazing. There are different types of hostels for different types of travelers and budgets. If you have a bit extra to spare, splurging on the right hostel can be totally worth it.
  3. What freebies does the hostel have? Our favorite travel hack is finding hostels that offer free breakfast. Yes, this cost is typically included in the price, but sometimes that small hike in cost for a dorm with a free brekkie is cheaper than paying for breakfast by yourself (especially in Western Europe or North America!). Other freebies to keep an eye out for is free towels, city tours, maps, lockers, locks coffee or maybe even shots!
Things DO go missing sometimes…

Psst! Heading to a hostel? Don’t forget to pack a padlock! It’s well worth having one so you can secure your locker and protect your stuff!

What to Pack for your Shanghai Hostel

We don’t often travel without these essentials.

1. Active Roots Water Bottle: Hostels are increasingly meeting backpackers’ demands for potable (drinkable) water. Now is better than ever to start traveling with a reusable water bottle! Aside from cutting down single-use plastic, The Active Roots stainless steel water bottle will also keep your favorite beverage hot or cold all day long and it’s sized perfectly to fit an entire bottle of wine. ‘Nuff said.

2. Noise-Cancelling Earbuds: You’ll have some pretty sorry roommates – it happens from time to time. These noise-canceling earbuds help mitigate the damage. The comfortable and ergonomic design won’t hurt your ears while sleeping no matter how long you keep them in. Increase the quality of your sleep by listening to some relaxing music at night or just enjoy the silence.

3. Padlock: Unless the thief literally spends hours to figure out the code (he won’t), this three-digit padlock is going to keep your stuff safe. It’s great for keeping the zipper of your backpack secured or your locker properly closed. This basic and very flexible padlock saves you from having your essentials stolen. Definitely a must-have for every backpacker!

4. Sea to Summit Travel Soap: Soap can be very useful on long travels, but it’s hard to carry around. It might get taken off you at the airport or leak in your backpack. This Sea to Summit Travel Soap is the perfect solution for every backpacker with a thought for hygiene. The dry soap is super handy, and it definitely won’t leak (there’s nothing to leak). One box contains 50 leaves of soap, making sure that you’ll be clean, even on longer adventures.

5. Active Roots Microfiber Towel: Quick-drying, lightweight, super soft and compact – the Active Roots Microfiber Towel is essential for every backpacker. No need to use the scabby towels your hostel may or may not provide! Use it for drying off, as a yoga towel or even as a picnic blanket – the options don’t stop here. Made of antibacterial material, you don’t have to worry about your towel starting to smell unpleasant either after lots of use.

6. Power Bank: Showing up to a new city, after a 14-hour bus ride, with a dead phone and no directions to your hostel is a half-awake, messy nightmare. Putting a good power bank in your pack is the best and easiest way to keep your important electronics charged up for those long travel and sightseeing days.
