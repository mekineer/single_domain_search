    Does Luxury Backpackers have wi-fi?

    Wi-fi is available at Luxury Backpackers. View more amenities here.

    Is there an airport shuttle for Luxury Backpackers?

    Airport transfers are available. Please visit Luxury Backpackers on Agoda for rates and details. View more amenities here.

    Does Luxury Backpackers have a 24 hour front desk?

    For your convenience, the Luxury Backpackers reception is available 24/7. View more amenities here.

    Does Luxury Backpackers have free in-room wi-fi?

    Is there room service at Luxury Backpackers?

    Luxury Backpackers has room service. View more in-house services here.

    Is there currency exchange service at Luxury Backpackers?

    There is a currency exchange right on the premises at Luxury Backpackers. View more amenities here.

    What time is check-in and check-out at Luxury Backpackers?

    Check-in is from 02:00 PM, and check-out is until 12:00 PM. You may request early check-in or late check-out during booking, subject to availability. Guests checking in or out before or after the designated periods may be charged an additional fee. The hotel offers luggage storage to guests for before check-in and after check-out. The front desk is always open, day or night.

    How do I get to Luxury Backpackers?

    Luxury Backpackers is easy to access from the airport. In fact, you can even book your airport transfer in advance for greater peace of mind with the additional charge of 18 USD. Just use the "Let us know what you need" section of the booking page to let the hotel know you want a ride, and they will contact you via the Agoda messaging service. A stay at Luxury Backpackers also comes with easy access to the neighborhoods around the hotel through the convenient shuttle service offered at the hotel. If you're looking for an easy way to get around Hanoi with private transportation, the hotel can arrange both taxis or car rental.

    What is the smoking policy at Luxury Backpackers?

    Luxury Backpackers does not permit smoking in any of its rooms or indoor areas. The property offers its guests both smoking and non-smoking rooms. You can request your preferred room type during booking, but please keep in mind that rooms are subject to availability.

    Best Agoda deal in Hanoi, absolute steal! ”

    Hadn’t used Agoda since in Australia, thought I’d give it a punt after a very hot and busy room in the VBH two doors down. On offer for $6 AUD a night which is a fantastic deal. Stayed in the ten bed mixed, each bunk has its own privacy curtain, with power points and reading light next to the bed. The room is constantly air conditioned, with two fans to circulate it round the room - a great addition after the 40c Hanoi heat. My mate who is 6ft 4 had no issue with the length of his bed. WiFi available in the dorm rooms, a little intermittent at times but nothing that caused an issue. As with many places in Vietnam, shower and toilet in the same room but not a problem as very clean and good locks. 2 “wet rooms” in the 10 bed share. We’d already booked on to a Ha Long Bay tour, enjoyed it so much came straight back after the tour. Bar (fridge) is reasonably priced, big Dasani water @10k, Hanoi bottled beer @30k. Free breakfast, as with other hostels in Vietnam, some form of eggs and a baguette. (Bacon/sausage available for meat eaters) Tea, Coffee and fresh fruit on offer as well as cooked. Vietnamese breakfast option available too but didn’t have it. Reasonably priced food available on full menu throughout the day too. 10 minute walk from beer corner. Cannot recommend this hostel enough, good on you Luxury Backpacker Hanoi

    High standards”

    Short story: book this place. Some more detail... You'll find all the essential facilities provided, and at a higher standard than I've found at any other backpackers. These include: comfy, spacious beds, huge under-bed lockers (free of charge), two bathrooms per dorm, complimentary breakfast (7:00 - 9:30), fast laundry, and a wealth of tour and transport services. Cleanliness: couldn't be better, really. The rooms/bathrooms are cleaned daily, around 10am, and the lobby/dining room is always clean and tidy. Note: you can have your bedding and towels changed daily, you need only ask. Service: among the exceptional few, in my experience; warm, welcoming, friendly, attentive but not overbearing - happy to help. There are plenty of staff around during the day, so it's never difficult getting info, advice, or making bookings. Sam, Hien, James, and Amy will be your main points of contact. They're lovely people, and have have helped me alot with good, honest advice and flexibility to my plans. Location: great for travellers - Hoan Kiem/Old Quarter, close to everything. I walk everywhere I need to go in the area, Grab-bikes are cheap for further journeys. Far too many great places for your food/drinks in the area, I won't even attempt a list, but special mention goes to the Bun Cha vendor on the same street (more on a side street) as the backpackers - right next to a hair salon. Probably the best Bun Cha I've had, certainly the cheapest (30,000đ), they're only open from morning til midday/1 - a local favourite. So, top spot - you won't be disappointed.
