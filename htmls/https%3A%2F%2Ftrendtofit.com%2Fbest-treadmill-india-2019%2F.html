Indian Buying Guide to Treadmills

Here is what you need to Look for when buying a treadmill in India.

As I told you above there is nothing like the best treadmill. The best treadmill is the only one that can fulfil your goals. Here, you go and find the best treadmill in India.

1. Brand

A brand is the most important feature to look for while buying a treadmill. And also, one other important thing you should keep in mind it’s not always true that most expensive treadmill brands come with all the features you need.

Therefore, you better check out all the specifications carefully before you invest in a brand. You must check out the brand name, its reputation, customer reviews about it.

Be double-sure before you invest in a brand. Always choose the right kind of brand which determines product quality and service levels.

I have enlisted top-level treadmill brands in India below based on which, you can make a wise decision for you.

2. Budget

Budget is another big factor when it comes to buying a piece of fitness equipment. It’s so confusing because, at the same time, you have to select an ideal treadmill that not only fulfils your requirement but also falls under your budget.

And you may wonder which is the best treadmill for the money.

Usually, a decent treadmill comes with a range of around 25,000 to 1,00,000. Though there are affordable so-so models that fall under 20,000. Keep in mind those may not last as long, especially if your purpose is running and you have several people at home using the treadmill.

And, most of the treadmills under 20,000 don’t have track cushioning which is a must-have feature if you have joint pain or purchasing the treadmill for your family and one of your family members suffers from joint, back or knee pain. You can say, it’s little difficult to find the feature in treadmills under 20,000.

But if your purpose is just walking and home cardio, there are models which are very good and fit your budget too. You can get those treadmills under 20000 too.

Usually, you can find the best treadmills between 30,000 to 40,000 in India for running purpose and middle and intense level cardio.

And if you want more horsepower or you are overweight you can find very good treadmills above 40,000. This article covers the treadmills of every type and budget.

3. Treadmill Size

When buying a treadmill, make sure its size fits for the space you have dedicated to placing it at home.

Usually, home treadmills are about 7′ long and 3′ wide.

Still, some other treadmills require a lot of room when they’re in use. So, you should go for a foldable treadmill that you can fold after daily workouts.

You can find foldable home treadmills at every quality level.

4. Belt Length & Desk Space

The belt length & deck space is a very important factor to consider if you are looking for an ideal treadmill. The running deck is the area on which you run. It has a belt and a roller on each end.

To run easily and faster on the treadmill, you should go for a wider and a longer belt.

The ideal size of the belt is:

  • At least 55 inches long and 22 inches wide if your purpose is only running.
  • If you are over 6 feet tall, you would require a 60-inch belt for running and at least a 54-inch belt for walking.
  • But for daily running, the belt size of the treadmill is good if it is at least 48 inches long and 18 inches wide.

5. Track Cushioning

Treadmill decks have a cushion to help protect your joints from the impact of exercise. Cushioning not only reduces the risk of injury but also promotes stamina.

Customers who bought treadmills with track cushioning found this feature very helpful. Cushioning allows making it soft on the knees. Despite having knee pain, they were able to do the cardio exercise easily.

But it’s also not a good idea to go for a treadmill which has too much cushion. So, the best idea would be to consider those treadmills which have a middle level of cushioning.

Advanced & expensive treadmills have decks featuring adjustable cushioning. The more expensive the treadmill is, the better the cushioning feature will have!

6. Display

The display is like a monitor that enables you to see all the specifications and parameters such as your calorie burn, heart rate while exercising.

Make sure your treadmill has the feature because it will help you analyze your workout properly.

Usually, a big display of around 5.5 inches increases the watching ability. But some people find it distracting to measure the progress in the middle of the exercise.

But still, it’s a good and necessary feature in a treadmill.

7. Incline

The feature incline on treadmills is designed to make your exercise more interesting and easier. It varies your ride, enables you to burn calories faster and provides a support system to build your muscles better.

There can be two types of inclines in a treadmill – Auto incline and manual incline.

Treadmills with auto incline come with a higher price and automatically detects your speed plus decides to incline the floor for a better workout.

But, you may wonder how much incline would you use?

Mostly home treadmills have maximum inclines of 10 per cent, 15 per cent, and 20 per cent. You can aim for 10 – 15%.

(Click Infographic to enlarge.)

8. Motor power

A treadmill’s motor is the machine which is there to power the track. Its power is measured in terms of horsepower (HP) or continuous horsepower (CHP).

Usually, the power of a home treadmill’s motor is somewhere between 2.25 and 4.25 CHP.

But, the question is how much treadmill motor power do you need?

Well, it depends on your treadmill workout goals and your body weight. If you are a person with an average weight you need:

  • 2.0 CHP for walking
  • 2.5 CHP for jogging
  • 3.0 CHP or higher for running.

But generally,

  • for the daily running and basic home cardio, usually 2.0 HP DC motor is sufficient.
  • If you are overweight, you would need a more powerful treadmill. You’ll have to pay more in that case.

Remember, the more powerful the treadmill is, the more expensive & efficient it would be!

9. Track speed

If you are buying a treadmill, you might not buy it only for jogging and walking.

And if you want to run on the treadmill, you should get a treadmill the speed of which is 10 mph or higher.

10. Programs

Modern treadmills usually come with built-in programs. For example, by using such a program you would be able to lose weight easier. Great! Isn’t it?

So, you can choose from these programs according to your requirements and prepare your regime.

11. Weight Capacity

You need to be extra careful here as this is the most important feature in a treadmill to look out for.

To look for the weight capacity in a treadmill simply means to look for a treadmill having higher weight capacity than your body weight.

You must always choose a treadmill that has a user weight capacity of at least 20 Kg more than your current weight because of the impact weight increases during running.

For example, if your weight is 75 kg, you can’t use a treadmill having a weight capacity of 75 kg or less. You should buy a treadmill of around 100 kg of weight capacity or more.

You need to invest in a higher-end product then and you have to pay more.

12. Power Use

The efficiency of the equipment is determined by how much it may be advantageous to you and can save your pocket. When you use a treadmill, it’s gonna increase your electricity bill for sure.

In that case, you need a treadmill that saves up on your budget along with your calories.

You need to check out the power usage of a treadmill and make sure that the motor of your treadmill uses lesser power.

Modern treadmills come with green DC motors which are noise-free and saves power.

You don’t need to worry here.

This list includes only those treadmills which are having energy-saving and noise-free motors.

13. Extra Features

Tracking is another imp feature you should consider while buying a treadmill.

Trackers and sensors such as heart rate monitors, calories, BMI, and pulse track your progress & other things related to your body.

Extra features are to make sure that you don’t stress yourself too much.

14. Reviews

You must read customer reviews about any fitness equipment or any kind of product you order online. That is the ease of online purchase which you can not get if you buy from the store.

And it’s equally important to read honest reviews. For that, you need to check whether it’s ‘verified purchase’.

The treadmills reviewed in this article include feedback from customers and are as per the deep research. You can also check yourself on Amazon by clicking the ‘buy’ button.

15. Warranties

A well-known and good brand provides different kinds of customer support and warranties. When you buy a treadmill, make sure they are providing warranties. Otherwise, drop the idea and don’t go for the brand.

These warranties from top brands include a warranty on the frame, parts, and motor.

16. Safety

There’s a very easy way of ensuring the safety of a treadmill. Just look to see if there’s a safety key near the control panel.

For safety purposes, there must be an auto-stop feature on your treadmill. Especially if older adults or who are infirm are going to buy the treadmill. It’s also important to those with pets or young children.

Auto-stop is usually controlled with a key. A safety key is a plug that is used to pull to immediately stop the treadmill.

It becomes necessary for situations when you can’t operate the control panel properly.

17. Try It Before You Buy It

It would be wise to try a treadmill yourself before inviting it to your home. Because you may find most treadmills fine with the kind of design and the features that they come with.

But the possibility is you buy it and later you come to know that it doesn’t have that particular feature which you were looking for or you are not comfortable with its design.

So, be sure that you are buying a tried and tested model of a treadmill.

Note: Go to test in the store but buy your treadmill online!

5. How to Use the Treadmill Safely (Safety Tips)

This is one of the treadmill FAQs – can treadmill cause knee pain?

It is clear treadmill running is safe if used correctly. You get knee pain because of the treadmill if you are increasing the intensity of your workout.

In the case when you increase the treadmill’s speed, it puts more pressure on your knees, which may result in knee pain and some irritation in the knee joint. Source-Healthline.

So, what should you do? You just need to take these steps before you start on a treadmill:

  • Before you start, ask a trainer or professional assistance. And if you are in any health condition, you must visit your doctor first.
  • Give proper time to learn how to operate the machine. Switch on/off buttons, know the control panel well, the safety key, etc. Be well aware of all the operating systems.

Note – Modern treadmills provide the facility for easy installation.

  • Shop the appropriate shoes.
  • Don’t step on or off the treadmill belt while it’s moving.
  • Warm-up and cool down.
  • According to researchers, if a treadmill has an auto or manual incline function, you should raise the grade slightly not more than 3 per cent. It can lessen the risk of knee pain. Because it reduces the shock on legs and knees by 24 per cent.
