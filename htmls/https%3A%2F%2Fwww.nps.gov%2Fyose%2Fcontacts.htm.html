COVID-19 Response

Following guidance from the White House, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, and state and local public health authorities, we are increasing access and services in a phased approach across all units of the National Park System. Before visiting a park, please check the park website to determine its operating status. Updates about the overall NPS response to COVID-19, including safety information, are posted on www.nps.gov/coronavirus.

  • Southern portion of Yosemite under a fire advisory; Mariposa Grove is closed

    Alert 1 , Severity ,danger ,,Southern portion of Yosemite under a fire advisory; Mariposa Grove is closedA fire advisory is in effect south of Chinquapin (this includes Wawona). An advisory message is a notice of danger in the future; it is not an evacuation order, but you should GET SET if an order is given. Mariposa Grove is closed.
    more

  • Tioga Road (continuation of Highway 120 through the park) is closed from 10 pm to 7 am nightly

    Alert 1 , Severity ,closure ,,Tioga Road (continuation of Highway 120 through the park) is closed from 10 pm to 7 am nightlyTioga Road is open from 7 am to 10 pm, with one lane open (expect delays). The road is closed to through traffic from 10 pm to 7 am. Closure is from near White to Porcupine Flat Campground. Call 209/372-0200 (then 1, 1) for current road status.

  • Phased reopening: Yosemite is open with reservations required due to COVID-19

    Alert 1 , Severity ,closure ,,Phased reopening: Yosemite is open with reservations required due to COVID-19We are working to increase access to the park in a phased approach. Yosemite National Park is open with limited services and facilities to those with day-use reservations, reservations for in-park lodging or camping, and wilderness or Half Dome permits.
    more

  • Reservation is required to travel through the park on Tioga Road (continuation of Highway 120)

    Alert 1 , Severity ,closure ,,Reservation is required to travel through the park on Tioga Road (continuation of Highway 120)Traveling through the park on Tioga Road or any other road requires a reservation. Highway 108 (Sonora Pass) is the closest alternate route over the Sierra Nevada in most cases.
    more

    Alert 1 , Severity ,closure ,,Se necesitan hacer reservaciones para entrar y manejar dentro del Parque Nacional de YosemiteYosemite est abierto con servicios y comodidades limitadas para personas que tienen reservaciones de da, reservaciones de viviendas/hoteles o campamentos dentro del parque, y permisos de Naturaleza (Wilderness permit) o Half Dome.

    Contact Us

      Contact Us

      Before contacting us...

      Have you searched the website to find what you're looking for? You can probably find the answer to your question more quickly than waiting for a reply. Some of our most common questions involve:

      • Camping (if you can't find a campground reservation, find out how to search for cancellations or information about camping without a reservation)

      • Can't find reservations for camping or lodging

      • Wilderness permits and backpacking

      • General hiking information and Half Dome permits

      • How to get to/from Yosemite when Tioga Road (Highway 120) is closed

      • Tire chains

      • When will the Tioga Road (continuation of Highway 120 through the park) open or close?

      Have questions about visiting in 2020?

      • Do I need a reservation to visit? (Yes)
      • How do I get a reservation? (There are a few different ways to get a reservation)
      • On recreation.gov what does it mean that reservations are not yet released? (20% of reservations are released two days in advance, promptly at 7 am)
      • Do I have to arrive on the first day of my reservation? (Yes)

      • How long is my reservation valid? (Seven days)

      You can find the answers to these and many other questions on our FAQ.

      Email

      • Ask a question or make a comment
        Please include dates or season of your visit, if applicable. We usually reply to emails within one week.

      • Request printed information to be sent by postal mail For example, trip planners, brochures, and other publications. We may not reply, but you should receive what you requested within a few weeks.
      • Lost & Found
        Please include your address and phone number; we will only contact you if we find your item or if we have more questions

      Phone

      General questions: 209/372-0200 (then dial 3 then 5)

      Open 9 am to 5 pm Pacific time (closed for lunch). If you're returned to the main menu, it means the ranger is already on the line (try again).

      If you have wilderness questions (e.g., hiking Half Dome, backpacking), you can call 209/372-0826, Monday through Friday (9 am to noon and 1 to 4:30 pm) to speak to a wilderness ranger. This phone is in service from March through September.

      Mail

      Public Information Office
      PO Box 577
      Yosemite, CA 95389

      Looking for a physical address? Please note that GPS units do not always provide accurate directions to or within Yosemite. Yosemite covers 1,200 square miles and does not have a single address. We do not recommend using GPS units for directions in and around Yosemite.
